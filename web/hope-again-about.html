Mission Statement

Hope Again functions as a serving arm of the church. We hope to demonstrate God’s love in action —

To assist those who are trapped in poverty, crime, and substance abuse;

To guide our brothers and sisters towards economic, social, and spiritual wholeness;

To encourage the larger Church in seeing the poor and disenfranchised through God’s eyes and partner in our ministry;

To build a practicing community of love and understanding between all peoples, taking seriously the message of Christ.

Philosophy

Hope Again assists those in crisis develop a plan, which, if followed, will lead to stability and self-sufficiency through comprehensive case management service. This service will strategically lead clients from a place of crisis and dependence on subsidy programs such as Temporary Aid to Needy Families (TANF) or Welfare, to stability, personal dignity and self-sufficiency.

Hope Again History

Hope Again, Inc., established in 1992, is a 501(c)(3) religious corporation founded by Carole Clarke. What began twenty years ago on a shoestring budget, in a small room at a nearby church, has grown to three resident facilities and a leased office space.

The agency was initially housed until 1999 on the campus of Fountain Avenue Baptist Church (now Hope Internationl Bible Fellowhip), and was a program of The New Church in Hollywood until August 2000 when it became an autonomous 501(c)3 agency. Hope Again’s diverse Board of Directors is comprised of clergy and laypersons from various communities and congregations throughout Southern California.
